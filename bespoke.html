The bespoke experience

Made only for you

A bespoke gown from TTMD is not purchased — it is commissioned. Every silhouette, every fabric, every crystal placement is chosen for your body and your occasion alone. Nothing is repeated. Nothing is rushed.

01
Chapter one

The Consultation

It begins with a conversation. You share the occasion, the feeling you want to carry, the references that move you. The designer listens — not just to your words but to the spaces between them. By the end, a vision is forming that belongs to you alone.

Duration
90 minutes in the atelier
Outcome
Design brief and mood direction
02
Chapter two

The Sketch

The designer translates the conversation into form. Multiple silhouettes are explored on paper — each one a different answer to the same question. You review, respond, and together you refine until one design emerges that feels inevitable.

Iterations
3–5 sketch rounds
Time
2–3 weeks
03
Chapter three

The Toile

The approved sketch becomes three-dimensional in cotton muslin. Every seam, every dart, every line is tested on a dress form matched to your measurements. The toile is where design meets reality — and where adjustments are made before a single metre of precious fabric is cut.

Fittings
2–3 in-atelier sessions
Time
3–4 weeks
04
Chapter four

The Craft

This is where the most time lives. The final fabric is cut. Seams are hand-finished. Lace is appliquéd by hand. Crystals, beads, and pearls are placed one at a time. A single bodice can take 200 hours of hand embellishment. There is no shortcut. There is only patience.

Hours
200–800+ depending on complexity
Time
6–14 weeks
05
Chapter five

The Reveal

The finished gown is pressed, steamed, and prepared for your final fitting. You see it complete for the first time. Minor adjustments are made. Then the gown is photographed as a fashion editorial — becoming part of the TTMD archive. Your gown becomes part of the story.

Final fitting
60–90 minutes
Editorial shoot
Included with every commission
Materials

The Fabric Archive

Every material in the atelier has a provenance. We source from the world's finest mills and artisan workshops — selecting only fabrics that match the standard our clients expect.

French Chantilly Lace
Calais, France
Gossamer-fine floral lace woven on Leavers looms dating to the 19th century. Each metre takes 8 hours to produce.
Lyonnaise Silk Duchesse
Lyon, France
Heavy, lustrous silk satin with a structured hand. The foundation fabric of couture bridal gowns for over a century.
Italian Silk Organza
Como, Italy
Sheer, crisp silk with extraordinary drape. Used for layered overlays, structured sleeves, and ethereal trains.
Murano Glass Beadwork
Venice, Italy
Hand-blown Venetian glass beads in custom colours. Applied individually by hand — no machine can replicate the variation.
Our promise

What bespoke means here

01
Singular
Your gown is made once. The pattern is drafted for your body alone. No design is ever repeated for another client.
02
Unhurried
We do not rush. A commission takes 4–6 months. The time is the point — it is what separates a gown from a dress.
03
Hand-finished
Every seam, every bead, every hem is completed by hand. No detail is delegated to a machine if a hand can do it better.
