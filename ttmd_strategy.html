Current site — three critical failures
Failure 01
No visual identity
Plain text navigation only. Zero photography, video, or visual representation of the craft being sold at premium price.
Failure 02
No client journey
A $20k decision deserves a story, a process, and trust signals. The current site has none of these. The bespoke page is completely empty.
Failure 03
No conversion path
Only a WhatsApp link as contact. High-net-worth clients expect a curated, private consultation entry point befitting the brand tier.
10 award-winning features
What the new $60k site delivers
Feature 01
Cinematic full-screen entry
Slow-motion 8K video of hands at work — needle threading silk, beads placed by hand, a gown taking shape. No text for 4 seconds. Then the brand name dissolves in, one letter at a time. The patience communicates price before a single word is read. Mirrors Dior and Valentino's homepage openers.
Brand
Feature 02
The Atelier — scroll storytelling
Scroll-triggered chapter narrative where each stage of making is revealed as a cinematic sequence: sketch → toile → first fitting → hand embellishment → final reveal. A running counter shows "762 hours in your gown." Forces clients to feel what they're commissioning before they even enquire.
UX
Feature 03
Private client portal
A password-accessed space unique to each commission. Real-time progress photography uploaded by the atelier. Fabric swatches, body measurements, private message thread with the designer. The client experiences the making process even when they cannot be in the room.
Tech
Feature 04
Fabric & embellishment archive
Macro-photography library of every silk, lace, tulle, beadwork, and crystal the atelier carries. Each material has an origin story — French Chantilly lace, Murano glass beads, Lyonnaise silk. Hovering reveals thread count, provenance, and tier. Like a jeweller's loupe, digitised.
UX
Feature 05
AI-powered vision consultation
Before the first in-person meeting, clients upload reference images, mood boards, and occasion details. A Claude-powered AI assistant generates a first-pass design brief and aesthetic vocabulary — silhouette language, fabric families, embellishment intensity. Two consultation meetings saved. Client arrives already half-decided.
AI-powered
Feature 06
The Archive — editorial gallery
Every completed commission photographed as a fashion editorial — not a product shot, but a story. The client's occasion, the inspiration, the challenge solved, the hours invested. Filterable by occasion, silhouette, year. This is the social proof system that justifies $20,000.
Brand
Feature 07
The craftsmanship clock
A live, animated counter on the homepage totalling every hour the atelier has invested in making dresses since founding. Changes in real time. Forces the visitor to feel the weight of "time taken." Reinforces the brand name as a philosophy, not just a label. Globally unique execution.
UX
Feature 08
Gated private appointment booking
A curated questionnaire gates the booking — occasion, silhouette preference, timeline, budget tier, occasion formality. Makes clients self-qualify before the designer's time is committed. The process itself signals exclusivity: the designer's calendar is precious, and access is earned.
Tech
Feature 09
Gown inspiration configurator
Not a gown builder — an inspiration engine. Client selects silhouette family, neckline preference, embellishment level, and occasion formality. The AI generates a curated mood board and fabric recommendation tailored to their choices. They arrive at the first consultation already speaking the designer's language.
AI-powered
Feature 10
Rental prestige — with upgrade path
The Truly Enamoured rental redirect is a significant missed opportunity. A dedicated Rental section on TTMD.com, with editorial photography and proper booking flow. Every rental page ends with: "Fall in love with this gown? Commission your own." The rental audience becomes a bespoke pipeline.

Haute couture benchmarks — what to steal
The world's finest fashion websites
Christian Dior
Split-screen dual world
Dior's homepage splits two equal worlds with a clean vertical divide. TTMD could split Bespoke and Rental with this treatment — giving both streams equal visual prestige from the moment of arrival.
Chanel
Typography as luxury signal
Chanel's site uses extreme whitespace and restrained serif type. The silence communicates that nothing needs selling hard. TTMD should adopt this: less copy, more negative space, and let the images do the convincing.
Valentino
Behind-the-scenes brand film
Valentino publishes mini-documentaries of each collection's creation. TTMD should film every commission reveal as a short film — the client's story, the inspiration, the final dress. Content that earns sharing.
Galia Lahav
Atelier process as hero content
The world's leading bridal couture brand shows needlework, draping, and hand-finishing as primary content. TTMD holds this same card — they just need to document and publish the making. The craft is the story.
Rolex
No e-commerce — by design
Rolex's site famously cannot transact — you must find a dealer. The site elevates the brand and drives to appointments. TTMD should do exactly the same: the only goal is the consultation booking, never a cart or price display.
Maison Margiela
Intellectual brand confidence
Margiela's site uses raw editorial photography and deconstructivist design to signal artisanal, rebellious luxury. TTMD's "neither trends nor forecasts" positioning can own this same intellectual confidence without apology.
Elie Saab
Macro embellishment photography
Extreme close-ups of crystal placement, beadwork density, and lace construction justify price instantly — no copy needed. Commission a macro photographer specifically for the TTMD fabric and embellishment archive.
Guo Pei
Cultural craftsmanship narrative
Guo Pei weaves Chinese embroidery heritage into every story. TTMD is based in Singapore — there is a rich Southeast Asian textile and artisan tradition no Western couture house can claim. Own that geography.
Prada
Single narrative hero video
Prada's soundless campaign video tells a complete story in 8 seconds without explanation. TTMD's hero video should be a single, evocative, cinematically composed sequence — not a montage of disconnected clips.

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–4
Foundation: brand direction & photography
Commission a luxury editorial photographer for a two-day shoot covering the full atelier process, fabric close-ups, and finished gowns. This content powers every phase that follows. Simultaneously engage a luxury brand strategist to define typography system, colour palette, and tone of voice document. This phase is non-negotiable — without it, the build is just a prettier version of what exists.
$12,000–$18,000
Phase 2 — Weeks 5–10
Core site: homepage, bespoke, archive, booking
Custom-built in Framer or Webflow (not WordPress — the animation fidelity needed is beyond WordPress without heavy overhead). Cinematic homepage with scroll-triggered storytelling. Bespoke commission page with process chapters. The Archive gallery with editorial photography. Private appointment booking with the questionnaire gate. Full mobile and desktop optimisation.
$20,000–$25,000
Phase 3 — Weeks 11–14
Client portal & AI consultation feature
Custom-built client portal using Supabase as the backend — password-protected per commission, with progress photo upload by the atelier, measurement records, and private messaging. The AI pre-consultation widget built on the Claude API: clients build their brief before meeting the designer, arriving with a vocabulary and reference set already established. Saves two meetings per commission.
$12,000–$15,000
Phase 4 — Ongoing
Rental integration & editorial content
Bring rental fully onto the TTMD domain with editorial-quality photography and a proper booking flow. Each gown page ends with the upgrade prompt. Monthly "Behind the Gown" editorial pieces for SEO and social. Instagram and Pinterest integration so every commission reveal becomes a content event that drives new enquiries organically.
$5,000–$8,000
Every feature in this strategy is buildable today — Framer for the site, Supabase for the portal, Claude API for AI consultation, and a professional photographer to unlock the whole thing. The craftsmanship is already exceptional. The website just needs to prove it.
